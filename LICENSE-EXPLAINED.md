# What you can do with this repo — in plain words

Most learners have never read a software license. Here is the honest version.

| Folder | License | You can... | You must... |
|---|---|---|---|
| `templates/` (GPLv3 folders) | GPLv3 | use, modify, ship products with it | keep your derivative open-source under GPL |
| `templates/` (MIT folders) | MIT (mirrors upstream) | use anywhere, including closed commercial products | keep the copyright notice |
| `app/` | GPLv3 | read, fork, learn, reuse | same GPL share-alike rule |
| everything else (prose) | CC BY-SA 4.0 | reuse in your classroom, blog, training material | credit the source; share your adaptation under the same license |

**Rule of thumb:** check the `LICENSE` file inside each template folder before building a commercial product on it. MIT = no strings beyond attribution. GPL = your code stays open too.

**Why copyleft (GPL/BY-SA)?** So that when this workshop's materials outlive the workshop, they stay open — nobody can take the commons private. That's a deliberate design choice, mapped to the [5R framework of Open Educational Resources](https://opencontent.org/definition/): you may **retain, reuse, revise, remix, and redistribute** everything here.

*This page is a plain-language summary, not legal advice; the license texts govern.*
