# Newsletters & Substack authors

Tags: `[level] [effort per issue] [free/paid]`

- **[One Useful Thing — Ethan Mollick](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/)** — `[novice-friendly] [10 min] [free tier]` — Why: the best plain-language writing on what AI means for work and education; evidence-based, no hype. Directly relevant to educators in this cohort.
- **[Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/)** — `[builder] [5–15 min] [free]` — Why: the most reliable running log of what's actually shipping in LLM tooling; his "things I learned" posts model the identify→use→iterate loop we teach.
- **[Interconnects — Nathan Lambert](https://www.interconnects.ai/)** — `[builder] [15 min] [free tier]` — Why: clear-eyed analysis of open vs closed models and RLHF; useful for understanding the open-weights rows in our matrix.
- **[Ahead of AI — Sebastian Raschka](https://magazine.sebastianraschka.com/)** — `[builder] [20 min] [free tier]` — Why: patient technical explainers of how these models actually work; the bridge from prompt-user to builder.

**Check yourself:** subscribe to exactly ONE (attention is your scarce resource — see [cost-heuristics.md](../decision-support/cost-heuristics.md); the same budgeting logic applies to you).
